Explore by route · 按路線探索

Seven routes where landscape becomes story.

Each route now has a practical visitor angle, a mythic landscape reading, and an image that anchors the place.

01 · New Territories

MacLehose Trail 麥理浩徑

Hong Kong's long east-west ridge becomes the atlas' main dragon vein: a demanding trail where reservoir water, volcanic coast, beach valleys, and mountain wind read as one continuous landscape force.

Route feel
Grand ridge journey
Visitor plan
About 16 km · about 6 hours
Myth layer
Dragon vein across the New Territories
Best moment
Clear cool days after the heat breaks

02 · Kowloon Ridge

Lion Rock 獅子山

The route reframes a familiar skyline as a guardian form. Reservoir paths and city-facing viewpoints make the mountain feel close to everyday Hong Kong while still carrying a protective mythic presence.

Route feel
Urban guardian ridge
Visitor plan
About 4 km · about 2 hours
Myth layer
Protection, identity, and collective memory
Best moment
Late afternoon when Kowloon lights begin to appear
View route See on the map

03 · Hong Kong Island

Dragon's Back 龍脊

This accessible coastal ridge turns the route name into the experience itself. The path rises and falls like a spine, opening to Shek O, Big Wave Bay, and a clean wind-water reading of island geography.

Route feel
Coastal spine
Visitor plan
About 8 km · about 4 hours
Myth layer
Walking along the back of a dragon
Best moment
Morning start, seaside finish at Big Wave Bay
View route See on the map

04 · Outlying Island

Lantau Peak 大嶼山鳳凰山

Lantau Peak works as an ancestor mountain for the project: high, exposed, and tied to pilgrimage movement between Pak Kung Au, Ngong Ping, temples, villages, and the wider island horizon.

Route feel
High mountain ascent
Visitor plan
About 4.5 km · about 2.5 hours
Myth layer
Source mountain and pilgrimage path
Best moment
Sunrise or cool-season daylight
View route See on the map

05 · Island Ritual

Cheung Chau 長洲

The compact island walk shifts from mountain trail to ritual route. Ferry arrival, harbour edge, temples, lanes, and Bun Festival memory create a protective village-scale story around movement and return.

Route feel
Harbour village circuit
Visitor plan
Compact route · half-day ferry visit
Myth layer
Temples, festival towers, and safe harbour form
Best moment
May festival season or quiet weekday wandering
View route See on the map

06 · New Territories Ridge

Pat Sin Leng 八仙嶺

Eight named peaks ridge across the Tai Po skyline, each one carrying the name of a Daoist immortal — making this the most directly mythic-named route in the atlas. From Tai Mei Tuk reservoir the trail climbs to Hsien Ku Fung at the high point, and passes the 1996 fire memorial as a quiet reminder of the trail's gravity.

Route feel
Named-peak ridge walk
Visitor plan
About 7.5 km · about 5 hours from Tai Mei Tuk
Myth layer
Eight Daoist immortals named across the ridge
Best moment
Cool dry season for clear ridgeline visibility
View route See on the map

07 · Lantau Island

Sunset Peak 大東山

Hong Kong's second-highest peak reads as a quieter companion to Lantau Peak's pilgrimage ascent. The plateau crossing from Pak Kung Au passes traditional stone huts and opens into autumn silvergrass, framing the route as a contemplative wind path along the island's high spine.

Route feel
Silvergrass plateau crossing
Visitor plan
About 5 km · about 3 hours from Pak Kung Au
Myth layer
Wind path companion to Lantau Peak's pilgrimage
Best moment
October–December silvergrass season at sunset
