龍脈 · Dragon Vein · Feng Shui

The Dragon's Path 龍脈

Five centuries of feng shui knowledge, drawn in light across the New Territories.

01 · 祖山

Ancestor Mountain 大帽山

At 957 metres, Hong Kong's highest peak anchors the entire feng shui grid as its 祖山 — the Ancestor Mountain from which all qi energy awakens and flows southward toward the sea.

22°24′N · 114°07′E 957 m

02 · 龍脊

Dragon's Spine 馬鞍山

Horse Saddle Mountain forms the primary 龍脊 — the dragon's backbone — transmitting accumulated qi energy along the central New Territories ridge, channelling it from peak to valley.

22°23′N · 114°13′E 702 m

03 · 明堂

Bright Hall 城門

The Shing Mun Reservoir forms the 明堂 — the Bright Hall — where water gathers and qi pools before continuing its journey seaward. Water in feng shui represents the accumulation of wealth and prosperity.

22°22′N · 114°10′E 494 m

04 · 出脈

Vein Outlet 西貢

The Sai Kung Peninsula marks the final expression of the dragon vein — where five centuries of accumulated qi disperses into Victoria Harbour, blessing the city that grew at the dragon's breath.

22°22′N · 114°16′E Sea level
