A coastal spine facing the open sea.

An accessible ridge where the route name becomes the experience — a path that rises and falls like a dragon's back, finishing at the water's edge.

Mythic landscape reading

Walking along the back of a dragon, in the literal sense.

The route's name is unusually literal: the ridge between Shek O Peak and Wan Cham Shan rises and falls in low humps that read, in profile, as a dragon's back. In the language of feng shui, a body like this — neither too high nor too straight — is considered an ideal energy conductor between mountain and sea.

To the south, an open ocean acts as the great water — a scale of qi that few city-side trails offer. To the north, the city of Hong Kong Island is held in the crook of the ridge, with the Tai Tam reservoirs forming a smaller bright hall (明堂) behind the line.

The result is a route where the dragon-body metaphor doesn't have to be searched for. It's underfoot, around the eye, and finished by a beach — a clean wind-water reading of island geography.

Walking the route

Four stages from To Tei Wan to Big Wave Bay.

A classic island traverse — most walkers finish with a swim or a coffee at the bay.

Stage 01 · about 45 min

To Tei Wan trailhead

A shaded ascent through woodland, climbing steadily to the first ridge platform and the trail's first long view.

Stage 02 · about 30 min

Shek O Peak shoulder

A first long look south to Shek O village and the open sea, with the dragon-body ridge laid out ahead of the path.

Stage 03 · about 1.5 hours

Wan Cham Shan ridgeline

The signature ridge: a series of small rises and falls along the spine, with sea on three sides for most of the way.

Stage 04 · about 1 hour

Big Wave Bay descent

A long woodland descent ends at the beach, where the trail dissolves into a swim, a surf-watch pause, or a coffee.

Practical notes

Before you set out.

The most popular ridge walk on the island side — early starts beat the crowd.

What to bring
About 2 L of water, sun protection, swimwear and a towel for the finish, light shoes with grip.
Water & food
Nothing on the ridge; village stalls and cafés at Big Wave Bay finish.
Mobile signal
Mostly reliable — the ridge sees the city's south-facing antennas.
Sunset & sunrise
A morning start finishing at midday Big Wave Bay is ideal; late afternoon brings strong sea winds.
Local etiquette
The beach finish is shared with surfers — do not wade across the surf line, and bring rubbish back to the bus stop bins.
