02 · Kowloon Ridge · 獅子山

Kowloon's quiet guardian above the city.

A short, accessible ridge that turns a familiar skyline into a protective mountain form — the closest reading of feng shui that everyday Hong Kong offers.

Mythic landscape reading

A guardian form watching over the Kowloon side.

Lion Rock reads, in profile, as the recognisable outline of a crouching lion — and in the local imagination, a guardian form watching over the densely-built Kowloon side of the harbour. The mountain body shelters the Wong Tai Sin temple complex below it and gives the urban slope a clear sense of orientation.

Within the language of feng shui, this is a classic protector ridge: a north-facing wall of stone that contains qi and prevents northern winds from running unchecked into the residential valleys. Local song, film, and civic identity have absorbed this form so completely that the phrase "Lion Rock spirit" now serves as shorthand for collective resilience.

On the trail itself the city stays in the corner of the eye for most of the climb. The ridge offers a short, tactile way to read the urban skyline as a landscape that has always sat at the foot of a guardian.

Walking the route

Four stages from country park to summit and back.

A compact urban-edge ridge — most of the day stays inside reach of the MTR network.

Stage 01 · about 30 min

Country park gate

Forested switchbacks from the Tsz Wan Shan side ease you into shade before the first city view opens overhead.

Stage 02 · about 30 min

Eagle's Nest Nature Trail

A balcony path with reservoir glimpses and the first long look down onto the Kowloon grid laid out below.

Stage 03 · about 30 min

Final ridgeline approach

Stone steps lead onto bare granite with the lion's profile rising directly ahead of the path.

Stage 04 · about 30 min

Summit and city view

The summit plateau opens to a wide city panorama; the descent retraces the ridge into the country park.

Practical notes

Before you set out.

A short walk, but the ridge is exposed — the city is closer than it feels.

What to bring
About 1.5 L of water, sun hat, light layers (the ridge can be windy), shoes with grip for granite steps.
Water & food
Country park kiosks at the country park entrance only; nothing on the ridge itself.
Mobile signal
Strong throughout — this is a city-edge route.
Sunset & sunrise
Late afternoon for warm city light; bring a headlamp if staying for sunset on the ridge.
Local etiquette
Lion Rock is part of Hong Kong's civic memory — keep voices low at viewpoints and do not climb on the bare summit rock.
