Mythic landscape reading

A walking version of the territory's main dragon vein.

Read at the scale of the New Territories, the long east-west arc of MacLehose Section 2 functions as the atlas' main dragon vein. Mountain spine, reservoir water, and volcanic coast align in a single moving landscape, so the route reads less as a hike and more as a passage through the geomantic body of the place.

The High Island Reservoir, with its dam and stone-pillar coast, serves as the bright hall (明堂) where qi is gathered. Inland, ridgelines transmit that energy westward across the New Territories; seaward, the open coast lets it disperse into the ocean.

Walking it is therefore not just movement through scenery but movement along a current — a quiet way to feel why this stretch of Hong Kong was historically read as the territory's primary protective line.

Walking the route

Four stages from East Dam to Pak Tam Au.

The classic Section 2 experience — reservoir, beach, coast, and finishing ridge in turn.

Stage 01 · about 30 min

East Dam start

A flat reservoir-edge approach across the stone-pillar dam, easing the body into the day before the first descent toward the coast.

Stage 02 · about 1 hour

Long Ke Wan descent

A short, steep step down onto Hong Kong's most photographed volcanic crescent beach, with hexagonal columns visible offshore.

Stage 03 · about 2 hours

Sai Wan & Ham Tin

Bay-to-bay coastal walking with two small village stalls, a famous suspension footbridge between coves, and easy swimming pauses.

Stage 04 · about 2 hours

Tai Long Wan to Pak Tam Au

A final climb away from the coast and a forest descent through Tai Long Au to the road end and the homeward bus stop.

Practical notes

Before you set out.

This is one of the territory's most exposed long walks — preparation is part of the route.

What to bring
About 3 L of water, sun hat, light snacks, sturdy trail shoes, a headlamp for late finishes.
Water & food
Limited — small village stalls in Sai Wan and Ham Tin only; carry the food you'll want.
Mobile signal
Patchy along the volcanic coast; reliable at the start, end, and reservoir crest.
Sunset & sunrise
Aim to start by 07:00 in summer or 08:30 in winter so the descent is in daylight.
Local etiquette
Pack everything out from the beaches; do not collect rocks or shells from the protected coastline.
