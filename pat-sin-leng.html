Mythic landscape reading

A ridge where every peak has a character.

The name Pat Sin Leng means Eight Immortals Ridge. Unlike a route that gains its myth through later storytelling, this one carries the myth directly in the map: each summit is named after one of the Daoist Eight Immortals, turning the ridgeline into a sequence of named presences.

Hsien Ku Fung (仙姑峰) is the highest point, named for He Xiangu, the female immortal in the group. From the summit the eye drops toward Plover Cove Reservoir, then follows the serrated ridge eastward as if reading a procession of small peaks across the sky.

The route also holds a sober local memory. Near the ridge is the 1996 fire memorial for students and teachers who died during a school hike. That pause gives the walk a real gravity: myth, landscape, and public memory sit together on the same line.

Walking the route

Four stages from reservoir edge to Bride's Pool.

This is a proper ridge traverse: a gentle start, one demanding climb, a linked summit sequence, and a shaded descent.

Stage 01 · about 1 hour

Tai Mei Tuk reservoir start

Begin beside the water and warm up on a gentler approach before the trail turns inland. The reservoir view sets the route's wind-water frame before the ridge climb begins.

Stage 02 · about 1 hour

Hsien Ku Fung approach

The main ascent climbs steadily through woodland and open slope. The reward is the highest summit and the first full view back across Plover Cove.

Stage 03 · about 2 hours

Eight named peaks ridgeline

The signature section moves from summit to summit. Treat each peak as a short pause point: look back to Tai Mei Tuk, then forward to the next fold of the ridge.

Stage 04 · about 1 hour

Bride's Pool descent

The day finishes with a long forest descent toward Bride's Pool. It is quieter and less exposed, but the steps can feel heavy after the ridge.

Practical notes

Before you set out.

Pat Sin Leng looks compact on a map, but the repeated small summits make it a serious day walk.

What to bring
About 2.5 L of water, sun protection, snacks, and shoes with grip for the descent.
Water & food
Tai Mei Tuk has cafés at the start; there is no reliable resupply on the ridge.
Mobile signal
Generally reliable on exposed ridge sections; weaker in the forest descent.
Timing
Start around 09:00 so the descent finishes before late-afternoon light drops.
Local etiquette
Keep voices low at the fire memorial and avoid smoking or open flames anywhere on the ridge.
